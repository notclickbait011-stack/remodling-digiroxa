How We Began

It started in a single workshop.

In 1998, our founder hung a shingle in Greenwich with a small bench, a set of chisels, and a belief that the finest homes are built by the hands that design them. The first kitchens were milled in a one-room shop; word travelled the way it does in Fairfield County — quietly, from one neighbor to the next.

Today Aldermere is still family-led, still milling its own cabinetry, and still measured by the same standard set at that first bench. We have grown into a full design-build studio of in-house architects, designers, and craftsmen — but the workshop spirit never left.
