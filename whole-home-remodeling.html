Whole-Home Remodeling

Your whole home, reimagined from the studs out.

When a room-by-room approach won't get you there, we re-plan the entire house — structure, light, systems, and finishes — into one cohesive home, designed and built by a single Aldermere team.

A Considered Approach

One vision,
carried through every room.

A whole-home renovation is a single, large act of editing — opening the rooms that should breathe, reinforcing what holds the house up, and threading consistent detail and material from the entry to the primary suite. Done right, it reads as if the house was always meant to be this way.

Space & Light, Reconfigured

We move walls so the light can move with you.

Older Fairfield County homes are often a collection of small, dark rooms. We re-plan the footprint around how you actually live — opening connections, repositioning stairs, and pulling daylight deep into the house.

  • Removed bearing walls
  • Reconfigured floor plans
  • New & enlarged windows
  • Vaulted & raised ceilings
  • Primary suite additions
  • Indoor-outdoor connections

Design and build under a single roof — and a single point of contact.

On a project this size, the gaps between architect, designer, and builder are where budgets and schedules go to die. We hold all three in-house, so decisions are made once and carried through to the last coat of paint.

  • One dedicated project lead

  • Engineering & permitting managed

  • Single point of accountability

Whole-Home FAQs

Questions we hear often.

Most studs-out whole-home projects run 6–14 months from the start of construction, depending on square footage, structural scope, and whether we're adding to the footprint. Design and permitting typically precede that by a few months.
Our whole-home projects generally range from $300,000 to $3 million depending on size, structural work, and finish level. Your fixed proposal itemizes every trade and allowance so the budget is clear from day one.
For most studs-out renovations we recommend relocating, since whole-floor demolition and systems work make daily life difficult. We'll help you plan the timing and, where the scope allows, phase the work so you can stay.
Fully. Architecture, structural engineering, design, permitting, and construction are all managed in-house by a single dedicated project lead, so there's one team and one point of accountability from first sketch to final reveal.
