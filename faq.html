Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you'd want to know before you begin.

A remodel is a significant decision. We believe in answering the hard questions plainly — about process, money, materials, and what daily life looks like while we're at work in your home.

If your question isn't answered below, we'd be glad to talk it through. Every Aldermere relationship begins with a candid conversation, and no question is too small. The answers here reflect how we've worked with Fairfield County families for more than twenty years.

Getting Started & Process

How a project begins.

A design consultant visits your home — at no cost and with no obligation — to walk the space, understand how you live in it, and listen to your goals. We discuss your wish list, your timeline, and a realistic budget range. You'll leave the conversation with a clear sense of what's possible and what your project might involve. There is never any pressure to commit.
The initial consultation is complimentary. Once you decide to move forward, we work under a design agreement that covers measured drawings, floor plans, 3D renderings, and a curated material palette. The design fee is modest relative to the project and is credited toward your construction contract when you proceed to build. This approach lets us invest real design time without inflating the cost of the build.
Design typically takes four to eight weeks depending on scope and how quickly selections are finalized. Permitting in most Fairfield County towns adds several weeks. Because we limit the number of projects we run at once, construction usually begins six to twelve weeks after the design and contract are signed. We'll give you firm dates well in advance so you can plan around them.
Timeline & Budget

Time and investment.

It depends entirely on scope. A bath renovation generally runs six to ten weeks on site; a kitchen, eight to fourteen; a major addition or whole-home renovation, four to nine months. Before a single tool comes out, you receive a detailed schedule with milestones, and we update you weekly against it. We build in realistic time for material lead times rather than promising dates we can't keep.
As a guide, bath remodels generally begin around $45,000; kitchens from $75,000 to $300,000 and up; additions and whole-home renovations from $250,000 well into seven figures. The range is wide because the work is bespoke — cabinetry, stone, and finishes drive cost more than square footage. Your fixed proposal itemizes every line so you can see exactly where the investment goes and adjust before we begin.
We provide a fixed-price contract based on a fully detailed scope and finalized selections, which is why we invest so heavily in design up front — it eliminates the guesswork that drives surprise costs. Change orders happen only when you choose to add scope, or when we open a wall and find a genuine hidden condition. In either case you'll see and approve the cost in writing before any work proceeds. No surprises on the final invoice.
Materials, Quality & Warranty

How we build, and stand behind it.

We specify and install to a standard built to outlast trends: full-slab natural stone selected piece by piece at the yard, solid-hardwood cabinetry, professional appliance lines, and hardware rated for a lifetime of use. We have long-standing relationships with the same mills, stone suppliers, and showrooms that architects across the region rely on, which means better material, honest pricing, and the ability to hand-select what goes into your home.
Our carpenters, cabinetmakers, and project leads are Aldermere employees — the people most responsible for the quality of the finished work. For specialized trades such as electrical, plumbing, and HVAC, we use a small roster of licensed partners we've worked with for years and who meet our standards. Every trade on your job answers to a single dedicated project lead, so accountability never gets passed around.
Every project carries a ten-year workmanship warranty on our labor, well beyond the industry norm. Manufacturer warranties on appliances, fixtures, and materials pass through to you in full. If something needs attention after we hand over the keys, you call us directly and we make it right. We also schedule a courtesy walkthrough after the first season to catch anything that settling or daily use might reveal.
Living Through Construction

Daily life while we work.

In most cases, yes — the majority of our clients remain at home throughout. For a kitchen project we'll help set up a temporary cooking and washing-up station so daily life can continue. For a full gut or whole-home renovation, living elsewhere is often more comfortable, and we'll talk through the trade-offs honestly during planning so you can decide what's right for your family.
Containment is a discipline, not an afterthought. We install zippered dust barriers, run negative-air filtration, protect floors and adjacent rooms, and seal HVAC returns in the work zone. The site is cleaned and tidied at the end of every workday, with a deep clean at major milestones. Most clients are surprised by how contained the disruption stays — your living space should feel like your living space.
Crews typically work weekdays from roughly 8:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., within the hours each town's ordinances allow. We respect your household — no early-morning noise, no weekend work without your consent, and a tidy departure each afternoon. Your project lead coordinates access and gives you advance notice of any louder phases like demolition so you can plan around them.
Entirely. We prepare and file the permit applications, manage engineering and any zoning or variance approvals, and coordinate every municipal inspection through final sign-off. We know the building departments across Fairfield County and what each expects, which keeps the approval process moving. You won't make a single trip to town hall on our account.
