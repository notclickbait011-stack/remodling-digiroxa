The Hawthorne Residence

Greenwich, CT — a dark, compartmentalized 1960s colonial transformed into a light-filled contemporary estate across a full 6,200-square-foot, whole-home renovation.

Get Free Consultation Call 800.555.1234
Overview

The owners had outgrown a beautiful but dated colonial — warrens of small rooms, low ceilings, and a back of house that turned away from a remarkable rear lawn. They wanted light, openness, and a home that felt unmistakably contemporary without erasing its New England bones. Over eleven months we re-planned nearly every room, opened the rear elevation to glass, and rebuilt the home around how a young family actually lives.

The Transformation

From compartments to a connected whole.

We removed three structural walls on the main floor, raised the rear ceiling line, and replaced a tired sunroom with a steel-and-glass great room that draws the lawn inside. The result reads as one continuous, daylight-filled volume — kitchen, dining, and living flowing together while still feeling considered and calm.

The Brief

The Hawthornes came to us after two years of living in a home they loved on paper but found exhausting in practice. The kitchen was closed off from the children. The ceilings pressed down. And the back of the house — facing nearly half an acre of mature plantings — was the darkest part of the home. Their brief was deceptively simple: keep the dignity of the colonial, but make it light, open, and unmistakably theirs.

The Approach

We began, as we always do, with how the family actually moves through a day. From there our in-house design team developed a plan that re-centered the home on a new open kitchen and great room, relocated the primary suite to capture morning light, and carved a quiet study off the entry. Structural engineering let us remove load-bearing walls cleanly, while a re-detailed exterior gave the colonial a crisper, more contemporary line without fighting the neighborhood.

Materials were chosen to age gracefully: rift-sawn white oak, honed limestone, warm plaster, and unlacquered brass that will patina over the years. Every mechanical, lighting, and millwork decision was coordinated by a single project lead so the home would resolve as one composition rather than a series of rooms.

"They didn't just renovate our house — they understood the life we wanted to live in it. Eleven months, and not a single surprise."

The Result

What was once a dim, divided colonial is now a serene, daylight-filled estate that still feels rooted in its setting. The family cooks, works, and gathers in spaces that finally talk to one another, and the rear glass wall has made the lawn part of the house. It is, by the owners' own account, the first home they've never wanted to leave.

Work Performed

  • Full gut renovation across three finished levels
  • Structural removal of load-bearing walls & new steel framing
  • New steel-and-glass great room addition to the rear elevation
  • Bespoke chef's kitchen with full-slab quartzite and paneled appliances
  • Relocated and rebuilt primary suite with spa bath and dressing room
  • New HVAC, electrical, plumbing, and architectural lighting design
  • Rift-sawn white oak flooring and custom millwork throughout
  • Re-detailed exterior, windows, and landscape integration
