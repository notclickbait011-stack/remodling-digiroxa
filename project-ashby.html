The Ashby Project

Westport, CT — three cramped, disconnected rooms reimagined as one expansive chef's kitchen and great room across an 1,800-square-foot, five-month transformation.

Get Free Consultation Call 800.555.1234
Overview

The Ashbys love to cook and love to host — but their 1990s floor plan fought them on both. A galley kitchen, a formal dining room nobody used, and a closed-off den sat side by side, divided by walls and wasted thresholds. In five months we dissolved those boundaries into a single, generous room built for serious cooking and easy entertaining, anchored by a marble island that comfortably seats six.

The Transformation

Three rooms, one great space.

By removing two non-original partition walls and relocating a chimney chase, we opened the entire rear of the house into a single connected volume. The new layout gives the cook an uninterrupted line of sight across the island to the seating and the garden beyond — so the host is never cut off from the party.

Back to portfolio

The Brief

The Ashbys had renovated cosmetically once before and regretted not addressing the layout. This time they were clear: tear down the walls, give us a real kitchen, and make the whole back of the house one room we'd actually want to spend our evenings in. Budget mattered, but so did getting it right — they were done compromising on flow.

The Approach

Our team studied the structure first. Two of the three dividing walls turned out to be non-load-bearing; the third required a modest beam, which we concealed flush within the new ceiling plane. With the boundaries gone, we designed a kitchen around a single large island — prep, seating, and gathering all in one — backed by a full run of floor-to-ceiling cabinetry that hides the pantry and a paneled refrigerator.

We selected a honed marble for the island and a more forgiving quartzite for the perimeter, professional appliances paneled into the millwork, and a layered lighting scheme so the room shifts easily from weekday breakfast to dinner for twenty.

"We finally have the kitchen we always pretended we had. It's where every single evening now ends up."

The Result

Where there were three forgettable rooms, there is now one of the most-used spaces in the house. The Ashbys cook together nightly, host without herding guests between rooms, and finally use every square foot they're paying for. Five months on site, on schedule, and on budget.

Work Performed

  • Removal of two partition walls and one load-bearing wall with concealed beam
  • Relocated chimney chase and rebuilt ceiling plane
  • Custom chef's kitchen with full-height cabinetry and hidden pantry
  • Honed marble island seating six, with quartzite perimeter counters
  • Professional-grade paneled appliances integrated into millwork
  • New electrical, plumbing relocations, and layered lighting design
  • Wide-plank oak flooring carried through the new open volume
  • Hand-set tile backsplash and unlacquered brass hardware
